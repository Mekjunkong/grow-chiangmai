# Grow Chiang Mai Market Research

Date: 2026-06-04

## Recommendation

Launch with subscription plots rather than land rental language. The customer is buying a serviced farm membership: bed access, local caretaker work, organic-method growing, live plot camera, cafe comfort, tool access, and harvest options.

## Pricing

| Offer | Monthly | 4-month plan | Notes |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| DIY Plot Club | THB 1,500 | THB 5,400 | Hands-on bed, shared tools, paid tool kit rental. Camera add-on optional. |
| Live Managed Plot | THB 3,200 | THB 11,520 | Best first offer. Includes caretaker care and live private bed camera. |
| Family Plot Plus | THB 4,800 | THB 17,280 | Larger bed, two named member passes, priority harvest scheduling. |
| Camera add-on | THB 350 | THB 1,260 | For DIY members who want live viewing. |
| Tool kit rental | THB 150/day | THB 600/month | Gloves, small hand tools, watering can, boots if sizes are available. |
| Farm workshop day | THB 900 to THB 1,200/person | n/a | Keep this as trial and lead generation, not the core margin. |

The 4-month plan uses a 10% discount. Four months is a good fit because many herbs and vegetables can be planned around a crop cycle, and long-stay visitors can commit without feeling locked into a year.

## Market Signals

- Thai government guidance says foreigners are generally not permitted to own land except under narrow conditions, while long leases are a separate structure. This supports membership/service language instead of land ownership or plot lease language: https://www.thailand.go.th/public/issue-focus-detail/010_012
- Chiang Mai organic farm cooking classes commonly price around THB 1,200 to THB 1,500 per person, so a one-day workshop around THB 900 to THB 1,200 is credible if the farm is still a pilot:
  - https://thaifarmcooking.com/full-day-cooking-class/
  - https://www.asiascenic.com/full-course-at-farm-only-morning.html
- Chiang Mai coworking day passes are commonly around THB 150 to THB 300, with monthly hot desks around THB 2,500 to THB 4,500. An AC farm cafe should not be priced like full coworking unless it has strong Wi-Fi, toilets, desks, and air filtration: https://thailandknowledge.com/best/best-coworking-chiang-mai
- Live cameras add real operating cost. CCTV guidance estimates occasional live viewing can use around 5 to 15 GB per month, and frequent live streaming can use far more. Start with per-bed limits, H.265 cameras, and a paid add-on if not included: https://www.3gmobilecctv.com/guides/sim-cards-for-cctv
- Rural Chiang Mai land can support agro-tourism if title, utilities, water, access, and building permissions are clean. Avoid land that cannot legally support the intended use: https://www.dotproperty.co.th/en/land-for-rent/chiang-mai

## Operating Notes

- Cameras should point only at beds, never at people, neighboring land, toilets, cafe seating, or public road areas.
- Start with one camera per 2 to 4 beds if individual live streams are too expensive, but sell only private bed views when the crop bed is clearly visible.
- Cafe should be small at first: AC room, iced drinks, water, clean toilet, member check-in, and a table for laptop use.
- Tool rentals should be simple, washable, and low-risk. Power equipment should stay supervised by caretakers.
- First pilot target: 20 paying members or 40 serious waitlist leads before committing to expensive cafe buildout.
