# Grow Chiang Mai Execution Kanban

This board reframes Grow Chiang Mai into the strongest first business for Chiang Mai: **paid farm-day experiences first, Harvest Bed Club second**.

## Product pivot

Do **not** start by selling a cold monthly vegetable-bed subscription, cafe farm, live cameras, or AI irrigation.

Start as a **Chiang Mai Farm Day + Harvest Bed Club pilot**:

1. Find one photogenic partner/rented site with safe access, water, shade, toilet access, and permission for hosted visits.
2. Sell three public Farm Day Pass dates to prove people will pay and show up.
3. Sell one Private Group Farm Day to prove higher-ticket demand from retreats, families, schools, teams, or tour partners.
4. Invite only warm paid visitors into the first 10 Harvest Bed Club spots.
5. Add cafe comfort, live cameras, AI irrigation, workshops, and larger expansion only after paid visits, reviews, group demand, and renewal behavior are proven.

## Why this is a better Chiang Mai idea

Chiang Mai already sells experiences: cooking classes, nature trips, craft workshops, retreats, family activities, and slow-travel moments. A Farm Day Pass is easier to understand and buy than a monthly garden-bed subscription. The monthly Harvest Bed Club becomes a trust-based follow-up product after guests have seen the farm.

## Mission 1 — Site + guest route proof

- Status: Doing now
- Value: make the first public experience physically possible and photogenic before selling too much.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - One candidate site has permission/use terms clarified for small hosted visits.
  - Water source, access, shade, toilet access, safety risks, and parking/arrival path are documented.
  - A 2–3 hour guest route is mapped: arrival, welcome, planting station, herb/tasting station, photo spot, hand-wash, checkout.
  - Real site photos are available for the landing page.
  - Weather/rain backup and heat plan are defined.
- Dependencies: local site owner/partner decision.
- Risk: beautiful site but poor access/toilet/shade makes guest experience weak.

## Mission 2 — Three paid public Farm Day Passes

- Status: Next
- Value: test whether families, expats, slow-travel visitors, and Thai weekend guests will actually pay and show up.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Three public dates are scheduled.
  - Ticket price is tested around THB 790–990.
  - Capacity capped at 10–14 guests per session.
  - Activity includes planting, Thai herb/crop story, photo spots, caretaker/operator intro, and small takeaway when available.
  - Feedback form measures review score, willingness to book again, group interest, location concerns, and Harvest Bed Club interest.
- Dependencies: site proof, host script, booking/lead capture flow.
- Risk: online likes do not become paid attendance.

## Mission 3 — Private Group Farm Day package

- Status: After first public-date proof
- Value: prove better cash flow with one booking instead of many single tickets.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - One repeatable group package is written for 8–16 guests.
  - Target price range: THB 7,900–12,000 depending on size/add-ons.
  - Thai/English host script, safety checklist, and activity flow are ready.
  - Outreach list includes retreats, homeschool groups, families, small companies, drivers/tour partners, and hotels/guesthouses.
  - One paid group booking or serious deposit is secured before cafe buildout.
- Dependencies: public farm-day reviews/photos, partner/referral channels.
- Risk: custom group requests create too much operational complexity; keep package standardized.

## Mission 4 — Harvest Bed Club follow-up

- Status: After paid-visitor demand
- Value: create recurring revenue only from people who already trust the farm.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - At least 10 warm requests from paid visitors or group guests.
  - Bed size remains limited to 3–8 sqm for the first cohort.
  - Weekly photo + field-note update process is defined and tested manually.
  - Crop mix is simple and locally sensible.
  - Caretaker hours per bed are tracked.
  - Member terms clearly say service/access/care/harvest options, not land ownership or guaranteed harvest.
- Dependencies: farm-day attendance, real site operations, caretaker rhythm.
- Risk: crop failure or caretaker workload makes monthly price too low.

## Mission 5 — Lead + booking operations

- Status: Blocked until backend choice
- Value: make the interest list usable for real bookings, follow-up, and later deposits.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Choose Airtable, Google Sheets, Supabase, or Vercel API route.
  - Store valid submissions server-side.
  - Notify owner when a serious lead arrives.
  - Segment leads by Farm Day Pass, Private Group Farm Day, Harvest Bed Club, language, area, and guest count.
  - Deposit/payment workflow stays off until site permission and first dates are confirmed.
- Dependencies: backend choice.
- Risk: browser-only localStorage is not real lead capture.

## Mission 6 — Postponed upgrades

- Status: Parked intentionally
- Value: avoid spending before evidence.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Cafe buildout postponed until 6 paid farm days or 3 group bookings show repeat demand.
  - Live cameras postponed until Harvest Bed Club members engage with weekly photo updates.
  - AI irrigation postponed until manual care workflow is stable across 10+ beds.
  - Workshops, transport, food, chef, photographer, and retreat bundles start as partner add-ons, not fixed costs.
  - Larger 2-rai expansion postponed until host hours, guest acquisition source, group conversion, caretaker load, reviews, and renewal metrics are known.

## Success gates

- Gate 1: one usable and photogenic site documented with real photos.
- Gate 2: three paid Farm Day Pass dates completed.
- Gate 3: one paid Private Group Farm Day booked.
- Gate 4: 10 warm Harvest Bed Club requests from paid visitors.
- Gate 5: first harvest cohort completes a crop cycle with renewal intent.
- Gate 6: only then test cafe/camera/AI upgrades.
