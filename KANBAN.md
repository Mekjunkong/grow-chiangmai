# Grow Chiang Mai Execution Kanban

This board reframes the idea into the smallest version that can actually run in Chiang Mai without overbuilding.

## Product pivot

Do **not** start as a full cafe farm with live cameras and AI irrigation.

Start as a **paid garden-club pilot**:

1. Find one simple partner/rented site with water, shade, access, and toilet access.
2. Run two paid trial farm days.
3. Convert the most serious people into 10 managed starter beds.
4. Send weekly photo + field-note updates first.
5. Add live cameras, AI irrigation, cafe buildout, and workshops only after members renew or ask to pay for them.

## Mission 1 — Site proof

- Status: Doing now
- Value: make the idea physically possible before selling too much.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - One candidate site has permission/use terms clarified.
  - Water source, access, shade, toilet access, and parking/arrival path are documented.
  - 10 starter-bed locations can be marked.
  - Real photos are available for the landing page.
- Dependencies: local site owner/partner decision.
- Risk: unclear land permission or inconvenient access.

## Mission 2 — Paid trial farm days

- Status: Next
- Value: test if people will pay and visit before asking for monthly subscriptions.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Two trial dates are scheduled.
  - Ticket price around THB 900 is defined.
  - Capacity capped at 8–12 people per session.
  - Trial ticket can credit THB 500 toward a managed starter bed.
  - Feedback form measures willingness to join, location concerns, and desired crops.
- Dependencies: site proof and simple visit-day SOP.
- Risk: people like the idea online but do not show up/pay.

## Mission 3 — First 10 managed starter beds

- Status: After trial proof
- Value: prove recurring revenue with a small caretaker workload.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - 10 members/deposits secured.
  - Bed size is limited to 3–5 sqm for the starter plan.
  - Weekly photo + field-note update process is defined.
  - Crop mix is simple and locally sensible.
  - Caretaker hours per bed are tracked.
- Dependencies: trial-day demand and site readiness.
- Risk: caretaker time/crop failure makes the price too low.

## Mission 4 — Lead + deposit operations

- Status: Blocked until backend choice
- Value: make the waitlist usable for real leads and later deposits.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Choose Airtable, Google Sheets, Supabase, or Vercel API route.
  - Store valid submissions server-side.
  - Notify owner when a serious lead arrives.
  - Segment leads by trial day, managed bed, family bed, area, and care preference.
  - Deposit/payment workflow stays off until site permission is confirmed.
- Dependencies: backend choice.
- Risk: browser-only localStorage is not real lead capture.

## Mission 5 — Postponed upgrades

- Status: Parked intentionally
- Value: avoid spending before evidence.
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Cafe buildout postponed until trial attendance + bed renewals prove demand.
  - Live cameras postponed until weekly photo engagement proves value.
  - AI irrigation postponed until manual care workflow is stable across 10+ beds.
  - Larger 2-rai expansion postponed until caretaker hours, crop failure, renewal, and visit metrics are known.

## Success gates

- Gate 1: one usable site documented with real photos.
- Gate 2: two paid trial days completed.
- Gate 3: 10 managed-bed deposits or commitments.
- Gate 4: first crop cycle completed with renewal intent.
- Gate 5: only then test cafe/camera/AI upgrades.
