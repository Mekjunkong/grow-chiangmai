# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Grow Chiang Mai is for expats, long-stay visitors, retirees, families, digital nomads, wellness travelers, and local partners around Chiang Mai who want a practical way to grow or receive fresh food without owning Thai land or managing a farm full time. Visitors arrive curious but cautious: they want nature, learning, local connection, and confidence that the service is real, safe, and respectful.

## Product Purpose

The website introduces a Thai-operated garden membership outside Chiang Mai. It sells the idea of small organic-method growing plots, local caretaker support, DIY tool access, workshops, natural inputs, and privacy-safe camera updates. Success means serious founding-member inquiries, not immediate online checkout: the first version should collect qualified waitlist leads and make the business feel thoughtful enough to discuss in person.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, generous, and village-smart. The voice should feel like a capable farm host: warm enough for families and wellness travelers, clear enough for expats making a practical decision, and respectful toward Thai land, local workers, and hilltribe/minority suppliers.

## Anti-references

Avoid luxury resort wellness clichés, generic green startup pages, fake eco claims, crypto-style investment language, foreign land ownership language, and templated landing pages with endless icon cards. Do not claim certified organic status unless certification is actually obtained. Do not make the farm feel like a speculative real-estate product.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the living field, not the business pitch.
- Make participation feel easy, but never legally careless.
- Show the human loop: customer, local caretaker, soil, crop, harvest.
- Treat social impact as operational evidence, not decoration.
- Let the waitlist feel like joining a founding farm circle, not filling a cold form.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard access. Respect reduced motion. Keep language plain and international, with short explanations for Thailand-specific terms such as rai, organic methods, and membership. Avoid copy that alienates Thai community partners or implies foreign control of land.
